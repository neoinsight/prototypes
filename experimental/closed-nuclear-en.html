Canadian Guidelines for Protective Actions during a Nuclear Emergency - Consultation

Health Canada is seeking stakeholder feedback on the draft “Canadian Guidelines for Protective Actions during a Nuclear Emergency”. This guidance is an update to and combination of two existing Health Canada guidance documents, the “Canadian Guidelines for Intervention During a Nuclear Emergency” (HC, 2003) and the “Canadian Guidelines for the Restriction of Radioactively Contaminated Food and Water Following a Nuclear Emergency” (HC, 2000).

Links to the two original documents:

  • Canadian Guidelines for Intervention During a Nuclear Emergency - November 2003
  • Canadian Guidelines for the Restriction of Radioactively Contaminated Food and Water Following a Nuclear Emergency

Themes to spark ideas

The draft "Canadian Guidelines for Protective Actions during a Nuclear Emergency" has been revised with a focus on:

  • Practical implementation
  • Taking into account lessons learned from Canada's response to the Fukushima-Daichi nuclear power plant emergency
  • Incorporating the most recent recommendations from the International Commission on Radiological Protection (ICRP) and International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA)

Comments and suggestions on the draft “Canadian Guidelines for Protective Actions during a Nuclear Emergency” are welcomed.

  • Send an email to the Radiation Protection Bureau for a draft of the "Canadian Guidelines for Protective Actions during a Nuclear Emergency

What we heard

Health Canada will finalize and publish the revised Guideline on the Radiation – Technical Reports and Publications webpage, as well as a summary of the comments received during the consultation.
